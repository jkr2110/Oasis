About the Food Oasis Project

March 1, 2014 by Oasis Team

On the surface, New York City appears to be a place where you can find virtually anything you need around every corner. A closer look, however, reveals that millions of New Yorkers have limited access to the most basic and essential commodity: food. Food Deserts, or areas with disproportionate access to healthy and fresh food, are a problem for as many as three million New Yorkers.

Food Oasis saw a huge opportunity for improvement in the way in which food deserts in New York are addressed. Thus, our dynamic website aimed to provide information about the entire food profile of a given location, including restaurants, fast food establishments, supermarkets, and convenience stores.

Community Commitment

March 2, 2014 by Oasis Team

Our aim was to raise awareness of what healthy resources were available to citizens within a reasonable proximity. Policy makers and interested business owners could also use this tool to make data driven decisions about where to find the most promising or underserved markets.

We strived to create the most interactive maps that would allow our users to explore food access across New York City.
