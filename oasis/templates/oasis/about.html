About Project Oasis

March 1, 2014 by Oasis Team

On the surface, New York City appears to be a place where you can find virtually anything you need around every corner. A closer look, however, reveals that millions of New Yorkers have limited access to the most basic and essential commodity: fresh food. Food Deserts, or areas with disproportionate inaccessibility to healthy and fresh food, are a problem for as many as three million New Yorkers who are overwhelmingly concentrated in low-income neighborhoods.

With this in mind, we set out to create Project Oasis. Project Oasis is a new, interactive, up-to-date assessment of food deserts and healthy food availability throughout New York City. Unlike past assessments, which provide a snapshot of the situation at one point in time, Project Oasis will be regularly updated as the city changes to be reflective of the current state of food availability, providing a relevant and useful tool for interested citizens and business people.

Using data drawn from a variety of sources, Project Oasis aims to reflect the complete food profile of an area, incorporating grocery stores, restaurants, bodegas, farmerâ€™s markets, etc. to create a unique Fresh Food Availability Index (FFA), quantifying the degree to which an area may or may not be a food desert.
